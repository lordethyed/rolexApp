History

1905

Hans Wilsdorf The history of Rolex is inextricably linked to the visionary spirit of Hans Wilsdorf, its founder. In 1905, at the age of 24, Hans Wilsdorf founded a company in London specializing in the distribution of timepieces. He began to dream of a watch worn on the wrist. Wristwatches were not very precise at the time, but Hans Wilsdorf foresaw that they could become not only elegant, but also reliable. To convince the public of the reliability of his resolutely innovative timepieces, he equipped them with small, very precise movements manufactured by a Swiss watchmaking company in Bienne.

1910

From the very beginning, Rolex has become a recognized manufacturer of high quality and precise timepieces. In 1910, Rolex became the first company to be certified by the Swiss Chronometry Society (COSC) for the precision of its watch.

1926

In 1926, Rolex created the world's first waterproof watch with a unique crown protection. This innovation led to the creation of the legendary Oyster, which has become Rolex's most famous and recognizable model.

1931

In 1931, Rolex changed the watchmaking industry again with the introduction of the first automatic watch with the Perpetual Rotor movement, which automatically recharges the watch when the hand moves. This movement is still used in many Rolex watches.

1953

In 1953, the company created the Submariner, the world's first watch that could operate at depths of up to 100 meters. In the same year, Rolex also released the Explorer, which became known for its ruggedness and reliability in the most difficult conditions.

Next
10 years

Over the following decades, Rolex continued to innovate with new watch models including the Cosmograph Daytona, Day-Date and Datejust. The company also continued to set new records, including the creation of the thinnest watches and watches with the most accurate movement.

Now

Today, Rolex remains one of the most recognizable and prestigious watch brands in the world. The company continues to create high quality watches using the most advanced technology and innovation. Rolex is also known for its work in preserving cultural heritage and funding cultural projects.

Logo

Who created the logo?

The history of the Rolex logo goes back over 100 years. It was created in 1905 by Hans Wildorf and Alfred Davies, who founded Rolex in London.

Logo

Initially, the Rolex logo was introduced as a crown pattern that was placed on the watch face. This crown symbolized the quality, reliability and prestige that was characteristic of Rolex products.

New Design

In 1925, the Rolex logo was redesigned into its current form. The new design was introduced in the form of a crown with five teeth, which became the symbol of the brand and can always be seen today on the dials and the screw cap of the watch case.

Crown

The crown in the Rolex logo represents power and elegance, while its five prongs symbolize the brand's five core attributes: quality, reliability, precision, innovation and prestige.

Now

The Rolex logo has gone through many changes since it was created, but the five prong crown has always remained the same. Today, Rolex is one of the most recognizable brands in the world, and its logo is considered a symbol of high quality, luxury and prestige.

Goal

The official goal of Rolex is to create and promote high quality timepieces. The company strives to achieve the highest levels of precision, reliability, durability and style in its products. Each new watch released by the company must improve or refine previous models, as well as meet the high standards and requirements set by Rolex.

However, in addition to this official goal, Rolex is also committed to maintaining its reputation as the best watchmaker in the world and creating products that will become true legacies passed down from generation to generation. It is also a symbol of status and prestige, and Rolex watches are seen by many as an indicator of success and wealth.

In addition, Rolex is actively involved in various sponsorship programs and charitable initiatives, such as funding research into the deep sea and ocean ecosystems, supporting environmental initiatives and various charitable foundations.

Peculiarities

1. High Quality: Rolex is known for its high quality timepieces, which are made from the finest materials and cutting-edge technology. Each product undergoes rigorous quality control to ensure the highest levels of precision and reliability.

2. Reliability: Rolex is also known for its reliability. Her timepieces are known for their durability and ability to maintain their look and function for decades.

3. Innovation: Rolex has always been at the forefront of watchmaking. She was the first to create a waterproof watch and also the first to create a watch with automatic winding. In addition, the company is constantly researching new materials and technologies to create more advanced products.

4. Prestige and status: Rolex watches are a symbol of status and prestige. They are often associated with success.

5. Classic Design: Rolex watches are known for their classic designs that have remained in style for decades. They combine elegance, simplicity and functionality.
